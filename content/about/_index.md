I am an Applied Research Scientist at [AllSci Corp](https://allsci.com/), where I build AI co-scientists for long-horizon scientific exploration in the life sciences. My work focuses on developing scientific reasoning systems that can decompose papers into hypotheses, research questions, and results, and combine retrieval, post-training, reinforcement learning, and evidence synthesis to support discovery. My research interests lie at the intersection of Large Language Models, Representational Learning, Science of Science, Information Theory, and Uncertainty Quantification. Previously, I was a Research Scientist at the [Center for Science of Science and Innovation](https://www.kellogg.northwestern.edu/research/science-of-science.aspx), working with [Dashun Wang](https://www.dashunwang.com/) at the Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University.

At AllSci, I have been working on systems that bring together scientific literature, clinical evidence, and hypothesis discovery into a single research workflow. This has included building agentic deep-research systems, retrieval and evaluation pipelines over papers, trials, and hypothesis databases, and infrastructure for making scientific outputs more grounded, benchmarked, and reliable. I am especially interested in how language models and scientific data systems can be combined to accelerate hypothesis generation, evidence aggregation, and decision-making in research environments.

My doctoral study was advised by [Dr. Hamed Alhoori](https://alhoori.github.io/), and my dissertation was fully funded by an [NSF grant](https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=2022443&HistoricalAwards=false). It focused on building a predictive modeling framework to investigate the [*Reproducibility crisis in AI*](https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/science.359.6377.725?casa_token=B4RgaLsweT0AAAAA%3AsuAiGo_9E7o0T0WkRQPxEQVn3-X9edRZ8OatwAxM2YEifI-S2bGXYNtTwNdRDg38zqEVTqBhLYe1SfE). Previously, I was a [Givens Research Associate](https://www.anl.gov/education/givens-associates) and an [Argonne Leadership Computing Facility Graduate Student researcher](https://www.alcf.anl.gov/alcf-student-opportunities). I have also given a workshop on using AutoML for Graph Neural Architecture Search at the [ALCF DeepHyper Automated Machine Learning Workshop](https://deephyper.github.io/events/workshop-anl-2022-summer).
